What is an exhaust fan?

An exhaust fan, also called a ventilation fan, removes stale air, moisture and odours from an enclosed space by drawing indoor air out and expelling it outdoors. Commonly used in kitchens, bathrooms, laundries, storerooms and commercial spaces.

Continue reading to explore types and buying tips.

Types of Exhaust Fans

Ceiling Exhaust Fan

Installed on the ceiling; ideal where there's no direct access to an outside wall. Air is channelled through a duct and expelled externally.

Wall-Mounted Exhaust Fan

Popular for homes; mounted on walls facing open areas to remove smoke, steam and odours without long ducts.

In-line Exhaust Fan

Mounted inside ducting for powerful, discreet ventilation — great for long duct runs and multi-room setups.

Window Exhaust Fan

Fits into window openings; compact and easy to install for a seamless look.

Features and Benefits

  • Air Expulsion: Removes humidity and pollutants for fresher air.
  • Improved Ventilation: Prevents mould and moisture buildup.
  • Low Noise: Many models operate quietly for bedrooms/offices.
  • Size & Airflow (CFM): Choose capacity based on room volume and air changes per hour.
  • Energy Efficiency: Modern fans use less power while ventilating effectively.
  • Automatic Shutters & Bird Screens: Prevent backdrafts, dust and animals.
  • Easy Installation: Most units include mounting hardware and instructions.

Takeaway

Choose a fan type based on installation location and required airflow. Match the fan's CFM and features to your room size and use-case for best results. Reputable brands offer models for various needs and budgets.

FAQs

What is the purpose of an exhaust fan?

To remove stale air, excess moisture and odours to improve ventilation and indoor air quality.

What to consider before buying?

Determine intended use (bathroom, kitchen, etc.), room size and required air changes, noise rating, and correct mounting cut-out size for a snug fit.

Do Crompton fans have bird screens?

Yes — most Crompton exhaust fans include built-in bird screens to prevent small animals from entering.
